K-Independent Metastability
on High-Dimensional Hypercubes:
Three Dynamical Regimes
and Their Vanishing Punctuated Band

This research establishes a rigorous mathematical theory of exploration–trapping transitions on the binary hypercube {0,1}^d, motivated by the long-standing question of whether punctuated equilibrium in the fossil record can arise from the geometry of high-dimensional fitness landscapes.

The project comprises three complementary components. The first is a large-scale Monte Carlo study (over 10¹¹ total steps across ~23,000 simulation runs) that systematically maps the (d, β) parameter space of NK fitness landscapes (d = 10–300 loci, K = 0–16) with two superimposed Gaussian basins of tunable depth β. This numerical work discovers three sharply delineated dynamical regimes—diffusive, punctuated, and frozen—and, critically, reveals that the punctuated regime occupies a narrow band that shrinks and vanishes as dimension increases (present at d ≤ 18, absent at d ≥ 20). A key empirical finding is that regime boundaries are quantitatively independent of the epistatic coupling parameter K.

The second component provides exact analytical results for the K = 0 (additive) case via birth-death chain projection and BEGK capacity theory. It delivers closed-form expressions for the rate function Ψ₀, the critical basin-depth parameter λ_c, and complete threshold expansions in two distinct regimes: Regime I (interior saddle, piecewise-smooth basins, linear barrier) and Regime II (boundary saddle, Gaussian basins, square-root barrier scaling β* ~ √(2e·d·log T)/s).

The third and central theoretical component extends these results to all K = o(d) via Dirichlet-form variational principles and McDiarmid-type concentration inequalities. Its main theorem proves that the rate function Ψ is K-independent to leading order: log E[τ] = dΨ + O(log d) for all K = o(d). Combined with the explicit K = 0 formulas, this yields quantitative predictions—threshold locations, transition-window widths, and their collapse rates—that are universal across microscopic epistatic architectures.

These three components are mutually essential. The simulations motivate and verify the theory; the K = 0 analysis serves not as a special case but as the “computation engine” whose explicit formulas, through K-independence, determine the quantitative behavior for all K; and the general theorem provides the rigorous bridge. The work identifies two new scaling classes distinct from the Ding–Lubetzky–Peres (2009) Curie-Weiss framework and demonstrates that concentration of measure on {0,1}^d is the mechanism driving the vanishing of the punctuated band—a phenomenon not captured by any prior metastability theory.
